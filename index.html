You've meant to write it for years.

You know things worth knowing. People keep telling you there's a book in there. But the blank page never moved. Not because you lack the ideas, but because you've never had the time, the structure, or a way to get your voice onto the page without it sounding like someone else. That's the part Coach Libra handles. She asks a few questions at a time by email; you answer with a voice memo whenever it's easy, in the car, on the plane, in bed, then send it back and go about your day.

How your book
gets written.

Five unhurried steps. Most of the heavy lifting is hers. You mostly just talk: record a voice memo on your phone, send it over, and confirm.

01

She learns your voice

Share a handful of things you've already written, and she builds a true print of how you sound: your rhythm, your turns of phrase, the things you'd never say.

02

She organizes your outline

A few good questions, answered however you like (even by voice memo), turn your idea into a clear, confident outline benchmarked against the best books in your field.

03

She constructs your draft

She asks questions you can answer by voice memo or email, then builds the manuscript chapter by chapter from your answers, in your voice. The part that's stopped you for years, handled.

04

You refine your draft

You read, react, and steer. Small nudges, not heavy lifting. The book bends toward exactly what you meant.

05

A polished manuscript

Rigorously edited and held to the standard of the highest-rated books in your category, then delivered, ready for the world.
