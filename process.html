What to expect

Your whole journey, start to finish.

Writing a book feels uncertain mostly because it's unknown. So here's the entire path — what happens at each step, the little that's asked of you, and the handful of moments where you're firmly in the driver's seat. No surprises.

Your call marks the moments only you decide.
00Hello

Your invitation & a warm hello

Once a place opens, Coach Libra welcomes you and invites you to tell her about the book you want to write — in your own words. No outline, no plan required. Just the spark.

You: reply however's easiest — type a few lines, or record a voice memo and talk it through.

01Voice

She learns your voice

You share a handful of things you've already written — emails, an article, a journal entry, a Google Doc, even a link to a talk you've given. Coach Libra studies them and builds a true print of how you sound: your rhythm, your phrases, what you'd never say.

You: send whatever existing writing is easy to find. When you've shared enough, just say “that's all.”

Your call“Does this sound like you?”She reflects your voice back to you. You confirm it's right — or tell her what to adjust — before anything is written.
01Behind the scenes

Building your voice-print

While you go about your day, here's the craft happening off your plate — turning what you shared into a faithful print of your voice:

  1. Read everything you sent — transcribing any talks or voice memos, pulling the text out of documents and links.
  2. Measure your fingerprint — sentence rhythm, punctuation habits, vocabulary, paragraph shape, and the small signature markers that are unmistakably yours.
  3. Gauge how much we have — from a first feel for your voice to a strong, clear read, so we know when it's faithful.
  4. Map your interpretive voice — your rhetorical moves, your tone and register, the way you use imagery and emphasis.
  5. Place you in good company — the acclaimed writers your style most resembles, as a benchmark to aim beside.
  6. Learn your “never” rules — the words and moves you'd never use, so neither do we.
  7. Keep your anchor passages — real excerpts that capture you at your best, to write toward.
  8. Train a guardian for your voice — a model that can tell your writing from anyone else's, standing watch over every later edit so your voice never drifts.
02Outline

You shape your outline together

A few good questions — who this is for, why now, what a reader should feel — and your idea becomes a clear, confident outline, benchmarked against the best books in your field.

You: answer the questions however you like (a voice memo is perfect). One thing at a time, at your pace.

Your call“Does this outline fit?”You confirm the shape of your book, or change anything — add a chapter, drop one, reorder — until it's right.
03First chapter

Your first chapter — a voice check

Coach Libra writes your opening chapter in your voice as a calibration: a chance to make sure the tone and direction feel exactly right before you go further.

You: read it and trust your gut.

Your call“Does the voice & direction feel right?”You confirm this is the book you mean — or steer it — and that sets the tone for everything after.
04Co-write

You co-write the rest, chapter by chapter

This is the heart of it. For each chapter, Coach Libra asks you a few warm questions; you answer with your real stories and examples — talking into a voice memo if that's easiest — and she writes that chapter from your own words. The part that has stopped you for years, handled — but the substance is genuinely yours.

You: just talk through each chapter. Your stories, your phrasing, one chapter at a time.

Your call · every chapter“Sound like you? Anything to add?”You lock each chapter when it sounds like you, or add more and she folds it in. Nothing moves on without your yes.
04Behind the scenes

The deep edit — quietly, in the background

With every chapter yours, your manuscript goes through a fifteen-pass edit, held to the standard of the highest-rated books in your category. There's nothing for you to do — but here is the craft happening off your plate:

  1. Developmental — does the book deliver on its promise, start to finish?
  2. Coherence — every chapter builds on the last, with no gaps.
  3. Pacing — nothing drags, nothing rushes.
  4. Argument & evidence — every claim lands and is earned.
  5. Prose — every sentence pulls its weight.
  6. Line — rhythm, clarity and flow, word by word.
  7. Grammar — clean and correct throughout.
  8. Copy — spelling, punctuation, consistency.
  9. Voice fidelity — still unmistakably you; any edit that drifts from your voice is reverted.
  10. Anti-slop — no clichés, no AI tells, no tics.
  11. Continuity — names, facts and callbacks consistent cover to cover.
  12. Fact-check — checkable claims trace back to what you actually said.
  13. Readability — pitched right for your reader.
  14. Peer comparison — measured against the best books in your category.
  15. Holistic read — read end to end, as a finished book.

You: nothing. Rest. The craft happens off your plate.

05Done

Your finished book

After a final review, your completed manuscript arrives as a polished Word document — every page in your voice. The book you've always had in you, finally written.

You: celebrate. You wrote a book.
