The best ideas in the room stay in the room.

That's the point of the room, and it's also the problem. The most clarifying thing you said all year is gone the moment the session ends. Meanwhile the public conversation gets shaped by people with more time to write and less to say. A book is the one venue large enough, and lasting enough, for what you actually think. And the only one that puts it under your name, in your words, on your terms. And she helps you make that book without adding a standing meeting to your calendar. You answer a few questions by voice memo between flights; your own words become the book.

Why us

Ⅰ

How your book
gets written.

Five unhurried steps that fit a schedule like yours. You write it by talking: record a voice memo on your phone, send it over, and watch your own words become a book.

01

She learns your voice

Share a handful of things you've already written: essays, memos, a transcript of a talk. She builds a true print of how you sound.

02

Your outline takes shape

A few good questions, answered however you like (even by voice memo), turn your thesis into a clear, confident outline.

03

Your draft takes shape

Coach Libra asks the questions; you answer by voice or email between obligations. Your own answers become the manuscript, chapter by chapter, in your words and your voice — drawn out and organized, never written for you.

04

You refine your draft

You read, react, and steer. Small nudges, not heavy lifting. The book bends toward exactly what you meant.

05

A polished manuscript

Rigorously edited and held to the standard of the highest-rated books in your category, then delivered, ready for the world on your timeline.
