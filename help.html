Help & how-to

A little help, whenever you need it.

Everything you send goes to one place: hello@coachlibra.com — just reply to any of my emails, or start a new one. Below: how to record and send a voice memo from your phone, and answers to the questions authors ask most.

❦

Record & email a voice memo The easiest way to work with me is to just talk. Here's how, in under a minute.

📱 iPhone

Using the built-in Voice Memos app
  1. Open the Voice Memos app. Can't find it? Swipe down on your home screen and search “Voice Memos.”
  2. Tap the big red circle to start recording. Just talk naturally.
  3. Tap the red square to stop when you're done.
  4. Tap your new recording in the list, then tap the ••• (three dots) and choose Share.
  5. Pick Mail (or your email app), address it to hello@coachlibra.com, and tap send.

🤖 Android

Using your phone's Recorder app
  1. Open your Recorder app — it may be called Recorder, Voice Recorder, or similar. Can't find it? Search your apps for “recorder.”
  2. Tap the record button and talk naturally.
  3. Tap stop (and save, if it asks) when you're done.
  4. Open the recording, tap Share, and choose Gmail (or your email app).
  5. Send it to hello@coachlibra.com.
Even simpler: there's no need to be polished. Talk like you're telling a friend — stumbles and “let me start that over” are completely fine. I listen for what you mean, and I do the writing.

Questions authors ask

Do I have to use voice memos?
Not at all. Type a reply, attach a document, or record a voice memo — whatever's easiest for you, at any step from start to finish. If talking is easier than typing, talk. I'll handle the rest.
Where do I send everything?
Always hello@coachlibra.com. The simplest thing is to just reply to whatever email I last sent you — but a brand-new email to that address works just as well.
What should I share so you can learn my voice?
Writing you've already done — nothing new to write. Old emails, an article or blog post, a journal entry, a chapter you started, a Word doc or PDF, a Google Doc link, even a link to a talk or podcast you've given (I'll listen and learn from it). The more I see, the more your book sounds exactly like you.
How do I send a document or a Google Doc?
Attach the file (Word or PDF) right to your email, or paste in text. For a Google Doc, paste the share link — and set it so “anyone with the link can view,” so I can open it. Lost track of what you've sent? Just ask me, “what have I sent so far?” and I'll list it back.
How long should a voice memo be?
There's no rule — a minute or twenty, whatever the moment calls for. Just answer the question I asked in your own words, the way you'd say it out loud to someone you trust.
Do I have to write anything myself?
No. You talk; I write — in your words and your voice, built from the stories and answers you give me. The book is unmistakably yours; the typing simply isn't your job.
How fast does this move? Can I take a break?
Entirely at your pace — one clear step at a time, and I only move forward when you're ready. Reply whenever suits your life; take a week off if you need to. Nothing is lost, and I'll gently check in if it's been a while. You can also ask for one step a day instead of as-you-reply.
Can I change how you work with me — tone, pace, how much at once?
Yes — just tell me, anytime, and I'll adjust on the spot. Want me more direct, or one question at a time, or gentler? Say so. The full set of dials lives on the options page, and the handful of things you can just say to me is on the shortcuts page.
What if I forget what I've already sent you?
Just ask — “what have I sent so far?” — and I'll list everything I have from you, by name. More handy phrases like this are on the shortcuts page.
How do I know you got my email?
I reply to everything, usually within a little while. If you don't hear back, no harm done — just resend it, or write to hello@coachlibra.com and I'll sort it out.
What happens to my writing — is it private?
Your material is used for one thing only: creating your book. It isn't shared around or used to write anyone else's.

Still stuck? Just ask.

If anything here didn't answer your question, write to me at hello@coachlibra.com — a plain “how do I…?” is always welcome. And if you'd like the bigger picture, here's the whole journey, start to finish.
