Working with Coach Libra

Just say the word.

There's nothing to memorize and no special format — you can always just write to me with whatever you need. But a few things come up often enough that it's worth knowing you can ask for them anytime. Here they are.

❦
Ⅰ

“What have I sent you?”

Lost track of what you've shared? Ask, and I'll list it right back — every piece of writing, document, link, and recording I have from you, by name. No more wondering whether something made it.

Try saying
  • What have I sent so far?
  • List what you've got from me
  • Which documents do you have?
Ⅱ

“That's all.”

When you've shared everything you'd like me to learn from, just tell me — and we'll move forward to the next step together. No need to keep going if you're ready.

Try saying
  • That's everything
  • I'm done sending
  • Let's move on
Ⅲ

“Here's more.”

Thought of another piece? Send it whenever — paste it in, attach a Word doc or PDF, drop a Google Doc link, or even a link to a talk or podcast you've given. The more of your real writing I see, the more your book sounds like you.

Try saying
  • Here's another piece
  • One more doc for you
  • Here's a link to my talk
Ⅳ

“Let me just talk it through.”

Typing isn't always easiest. At any step, from start to finish, you can reply with a voice memo and simply talk — I'll listen, capture it, and carry it forward in your words.

Try sending
  • A voice memo, any time

New to recording one? Here's how to record and send a voice memo on iPhone or Android.

Ⅴ

“Change how we work.”

Want me more to-the-point, one question at a time, or a single calm step a day? Just say so and I'll adjust on the spot — no settings to find.

Try saying
  • Be more direct
  • One question a day
  • Keep it simple

Curious about every dial you can turn? They all live on the options page.

Ⅵ

“This is right” — or “change this.”

At each step — your voice, your outline, your first chapter, every chapter — I'll check with you. Tell me it sounds like you and we keep going, or tell me exactly what to fix and I'll rework it. Nothing moves forward without your yes.

Try saying
  • Yes, that's me
  • Looks good — keep going
  • Change this part…
Ⅶ

“I'm not sure what to do.”

Stuck, unsure, or just want to know where things stand? Say that too. I'd rather you ask than guess — tell me where you are and I'll point to the one clear next step.

Try saying
  • What's next?
  • Where are we?
  • I'm not sure what you need

You really can just say it.

There's no wrong way to write to me — these are only the shortcuts that come in handy. Want to change how we work together? Those dials are on the options page. Whenever you're ready, head back to coachlibra.com.
