What's the book?

That's yours to shape. Many governance leaders write the story of their school system, or their own march toward effective governance — a turnaround, a hard-won philosophy, the lessons and the warnings they'd hand to whoever comes next. Some write the personal story of why they serve. Some write something else entirely. Coach Libra helps you find the shape, draws it out a few questions at a time, and keeps every word unmistakably your own.

How your book
gets written.

Five unhurried steps that fit around board meetings and the school year. You write it by talking: record a voice memo on your phone, send it over, and watch your own words become a book.

01

She learns your voice

Share a few things you've already written — a letter to the community, a board statement, a speech. She builds a true print of how you sound.

02

You find your shape

A few good questions, answered any way you want, turn your experience and hard-won lessons into a clear outline — the book takes its form from what only you can say.

03

Your draft takes shape

Coach Libra asks the questions; you answer by voice or email between meetings. Your own answers become the manuscript, chapter by chapter, in your words and your voice — drawn out and organized, never written for you.

04

You refine your draft

You read, react, and steer. Small nudges, not heavy lifting. The book bends toward exactly what you meant.

05

A polished manuscript — yours

Rigorously edited and held to the standard of the best books in the field, then delivered to you, the author, ready for the world.
