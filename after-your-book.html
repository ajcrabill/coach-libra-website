After your book

Your book is written. Here's the road to readers.

Getting a book into the world has three stages: writing it, publishing it, and launching it. The writing — the hard part, the part that's stopped you for years — is what we do together. When your manuscript is in your hands, that stage is complete and every word is yours. The other two are yours to take whenever you're ready, and you can do both of them yourself, for free.

You can do all of this yourself, for free.

The day your book is delivered, your portal opens a publishing plan and a launch plan built for your kind of book — step by step, with the actual pieces already written for you: your back-cover description, your launch announcement, your social posts. Most authors take it from here, no extra cost.

A few costs along the way are Amazon's, not ours, and they're small: KDP is free to join, an ISBN is free through KDP (or about $125 if you'd like to own your own), and you may want one proof copy at print cost before you go live. None of that comes to us — it's just what self-publishing involves.
